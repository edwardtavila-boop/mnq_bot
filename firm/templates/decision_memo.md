# One-Page Strategy Decision Memo

Every strategy that ships gets one. Not for compliance — for your future self, who won't remember why this strategy exists, only whether it worked.

```
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

THESIS (one sentence)
_________________________________________

EVIDENCE (3 bullets, numeric where possible)
• _______________________________________
• _______________________________________
• _______________________________________

RED TEAM'S PRIMARY DISSENT (verbatim)
_________________________________________
_________________________________________

[ ] Fixed — how: _______
[ ] Accepted as surviving risk — monitoring: _______
[ ] Overridden — rationale: _______

SIZING
Risk per trade: ____%   Kelly fraction: ____
Daily stop: -___R   Weekly: -___R   DD kill: -___R

"I abandon this by [date] if ANY of:"
• _______________________________________
• _______________________________________
• _______________________________________

MONITORING
First review: after ___ trades
Success: expectancy ≥ ___R, DD ≤ ___R
Failure: expectancy ≤ ___R, OR DD ≥ ___R

SIGNATURE: _______
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

Keep every memo. Read them quarterly. The patterns in what you got wrong are more valuable than the wins.
